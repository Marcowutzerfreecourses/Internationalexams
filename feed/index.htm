What is IELTS?
 

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is an English language proficiency test designed to assess non-native speakers’ English proficiency. The four skills of listening, reading, writing and speaking are evaluated using a nine-band scale with scores ranging from zero for minimal performance to nine for excellence. The test assesses all language skills: listening, reading, writing and speaking with equal weight given to each skill level. Candidates taking an IELTS test will be required to complete one or more sections from which their overall score is derived. This does not mean that all candidates need to take both parts – Listening and Reading or Writing and Speaking – but rather that only one part must be taken per candidate at each test session .

Importance of IELTS

The International English Language Testing System (IELTS) is a standardized test that measures your ability to use and understand English at work, at home or for study. Many companies and organisations require evidence of an IELTS exam as part of their selection criteria for employment or recruitment. If you are planning to move overseas, you might need to prove your English language ability with an IELTS certificate when applying for a visa. An IELTS certificate may also be required by institutions such as universities when applying for further education, or by government bodies if you want to apply for citizenship.
